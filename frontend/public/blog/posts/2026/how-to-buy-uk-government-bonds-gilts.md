### The Capital Gains Tax EXEMPTION on Gilts

The most appealing aspect of investing in individual UK Gilts for many high-net-worth investors is their tax treatment. **Profits made from selling a UK Government Gilt are completely exempt from Capital Gains Tax.** 

If you purchase a low-coupon Gilt below its "par" value (e.g., buying at £90 when it will pay out £100 at maturity), that £10 capital uplift is not taxed at all when you hold it to maturity or sell it for a profit. Be aware, however, that the regular interest (coupon) payments *are* subject to Income Tax.
