### Common Day Trading Tax Pitfalls

When trading actively, whether you are dealing with CFDs, spread betting, or buying underlying shares via brokers like Interactive Brokers or Trading 212, it's vital to know how tax applies to your actions. 

1. **Not Tracking Trades Properly**: Day trading generates hundreds or thousands of transactions. Without adequate reporting (such as our calculator tool), you might miscalculate your capital gains or losses.
2. **The "Wash Sale" or "Bed and Breakfasting" Rule**: If you sell shares at a loss and buy them back within 30 days, the UK's "bed and breakfasting" rule prevents you from using that loss to offset other capital gains.
3. **Confusing Income with Capital Gains**: If you day trade full-time and it is your primary source of income, HMRC might classify your profits as trading income rather than capital gains. This means you could be liable for Income Tax and National Insurance instead of CGT.
