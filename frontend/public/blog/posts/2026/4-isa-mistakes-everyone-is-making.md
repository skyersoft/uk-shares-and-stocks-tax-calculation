Individual Savings Accounts (ISAs) are the cornerstone of tax-efficient investing in the UK. Setting one up is easy, but optimizing it requires avoiding some common pitfalls.

{{youtube:x5L2s8tb6Sc}}

### The 4 Biggest ISA Mistakes

As a qualified accountant, Kiran highlights several errors investors make with their ISA portfolios:

1.  **Delaying Your Investment**: Time in the market is crucial. Missing out on the power of compounding by keeping your allowance entirely in cash ISAs rather than utilizing a Stocks and Shares ISA can heavily impact long-term returns.
2.  **Misunderstanding Withdrawals**: If you have a "Flexible ISA", you can withdraw money and put it back in the same tax year without affecting your allowance. However, doing this in a non-flexible ISA traps your allowance for the year.
3.  **Ignoring the Kids ISA Allowance**: Junior ISAs (JISAs) offer a separate, generous tax-free wrapper for your children's investments, shielding future capital gains as they grow.
4.  **Forgetting They are Tax Wrappers**: Don't confuse the account itself with an investment. An ISA is merely the protective wrapper; you still need to actively choose and purchase stocks, ETFs, or funds inside it to see growth.
