One of the main ways to profit from investing is to buy assets at one price and then sell them at a higher price. These types of profits are known as capital gains. As with most kinds of profits, they are subject to taxes. 

{{youtube:TLFG1_jFQ_Y}}

### The Impact of Taxes on Your Portfolio

Taxes can significantly impact the growth of your trading portfolio. Whether you are actively trading or holding investments long-term, it is crucial to understand how capital gains taxes work.

While this video from Charles Schwab discusses US tax principles (like short-term vs long-term rates), UK investors should note that the rules are slightly different here. In the UK, Capital Gains Tax (CGT) does not differentiate between short-term and long-term holdings in the same way. All non-exempt capital gains are combined and taxed according to your UK income tax bracket, offset by your Annual Exempt Amount. 

### Key Takeaways for UK Investors

1. **Calculate Accurately**: Your capital gain is calculated as your sale proceeds minus the allowable base cost of the shares.
2. **Utilize Allowances**: Always use your Capital Gains Tax annual allowance before it resets for the new tax year.
3. **Use Tax Wrappers**: In the UK, holding stocks within an ISA or SIPP protects those gains entirely from Capital Gains Tax.
