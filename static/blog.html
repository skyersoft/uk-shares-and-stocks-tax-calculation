CGT Rate Changes October 2024: What IBKR Users Need to Know

Published on October 30, 2024 • 8 min read

The UK government has implemented the most significant changes to Capital Gains Tax rates in recent years, with immediate effect for disposals from 30 October 2024. Here's what you need to know:

Key Changes:
  • Basic Rate Taxpayers: CGT on shares increased from 10% to 18% (+8%)
  • Higher Rate Taxpayers: CGT on shares increased from 20% to 24% (+4%)
  • Timing: Changes apply to disposals from 30 October 2024 onwards
  • Scope: Affects shares, ETFs, and unit trusts (not residential property)
IBKR Strategy Tip:

Consider splitting your IBKR exports by disposal date to accurately apply the correct tax rates for each period.

Read Full Analysis
Impact Analysis:

For a basic rate taxpayer realizing £10,000 in gains post-October 30th, the tax liability increased from £700 to £1,260 (assuming gains exceed the annual allowance).

Planning Opportunities:
  • Review your portfolio for potential loss harvesting
  • Consider timing of future disposals
  • Evaluate ISA vs. taxable account allocations
  • Assess spousal transfer opportunities
IBKR Export Considerations:

When using our calculator, ensure your IBKR Activity Statement covers the correct period and consider running separate calculations for pre and post-October 30th disposals.

STRATEGY GUIDE

Section 104 Pooling: The UK Advantage

March 15, 2024 • 5 min read

Unlike the US FIFO system, the UK's Section 104 pooling rules can work in your favor when calculating capital gains from IBKR trades.

How It Works:
  • All shares of the same company are pooled together
  • Average cost basis is calculated automatically
  • No need to track specific share lots
  • Simplifies calculations for frequent traders
Example:

Buy 100 AAPL @ $150 = $15,000

Buy 50 AAPL @ $160 = $8,000

Average cost: $23,000 ÷ 150 = $153.33

Sell 75 shares @ $170 = Gain based on $153.33 avg cost

Learn More
Advanced Tips:
  • Different share classes are pooled separately
  • Rights issues and bonus shares have special rules
  • Currency conversions use transaction date rates
  • Spin-offs require careful cost basis allocation
